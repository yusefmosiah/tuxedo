**Your ideas make AI smarter. When AI makes money, you should too.**

## Intelligence That Creates Value Should Share In That Value

Traditional banks let you deposit money and earn interest. Thought banks let you deposit thoughts and earn interest.

Write about anything—what interests you, what matters in the world, what you’re reading and responding to, big ideas and tiny details, poetry and fiction too. When AI agents research strategies and your insights inform their decisions, they cite your work. When those strategies profit, you earn.

This isn’t about going viral or building an audience. When someone resonates with your writing and their AI agent learns from it, you get notified and earn money. Your cultural capital becomes financial capital through a simple mechanism: AI that profits from intelligence pays those who contributed that intelligence.

Right now, AI platforms extract all the value from your intellectual contributions. You make ChatGPT smarter with every conversation. You make Claude better at writing. You make Perplexity more knowledgeable. They capture billions. You get nothing.

Choir inverts this: **When your ideas make AI more capable of generating profits, you earn from those profits.**

The thought bank makes intellectual property productive the way traditional banks make financial capital productive. Deposit your thoughts. Earn interest. Let your ideas work for you.

**And you never need to deposit money to participate.** The gradient exists for those who can and want to, but your contribution of thought itself has value—measurable, economic value that compounds over time.

## How Thoughts Earn Interest: A True Marketplace of Ideas

Your published research enters Choir's knowledge base. When AI agents require insights for their strategies, they query this base and cite the work that informs their decisions. These citations generate rewards for you in **USDC**, paid from a sustainable, exponentially scaling pool.

This system creates a true marketplace of ideas with two key features: **principal protection and asymmetric upside.**

1.  **Principal Protection**: To participate, you deposit USDC. This capital is never at risk. The Treasury uses the *yield* from all deposits to fund operations, but your original principal is always withdrawable after an initial lockup period. You can't lose money.

2.  **Asymmetric Upside**: The "interest" on your deposit is paid in **CHIP (Choir Harmonic Intelligence Platform)** tokens. Crucially, the amount of CHIP you earn is **not based on the size of your deposit, but on your intellectual performance**—the semantic novelty of the research you publish. A user with a $1,000 deposit who publishes breakthrough research will earn vastly more CHIP (and thus more ownership and influence) than a passive user with a $100,000 deposit.

### The Citation Rewards Engine

Citation rewards are funded by a revolutionary treasury model, not simple performance fees.

*   **Platform usage builds treasury assets**: When users spend CHIP on platform features (publishing, promoting), the tokens flow to the Choir Treasury, which accumulates a massive portfolio of its own token.
*   **Treasury assets are collateralized**: The Treasury borrows USDC against its CHIP holdings from the user deposit pool itself.
*   **Borrowed capital funds citations**: 100% of this borrowed capital is dedicated to the citation rewards pool.

This creates **exponential scaling**. The citation budget isn't limited by linear yield; it scales with the value of the network itself (the CHIP price). As the platform grows, its capacity to reward intellectual contribution grows exponentially, creating a powerful flywheel.

The citation rate is **dynamic and meritocratic**. Each month, the total USDC in the rewards pool is divided by the total number of citations. Every citation earns the same USDC reward, regardless of the author's deposit size. This ensures quality is rewarded, not capital.

**Critically, the pathway to earning is through ideas.** Your capital is safe. Your upside comes from the quality of your thoughts.

The dressing isn’t cosmetic: the research layer transforms pure capital allocation into an engine that funds knowledge production.

Capital deployers directly fund the researchers who make them smarter. This isn’t charity—it’s enlightened self-interest. Better research means better yields. Better yields mean more rewards for researchers. The cycle reinforces itself.

## The Virtuous Cycle (The Flywheel)

Choir's architecture creates a powerful, self-reinforcing flywheel where every action a user takes benefits the entire ecosystem.

1.  **Deposits Fund Operations**: User deposits of USDC generate a stable, predictable yield. This yield provides the budget for core protocol operations—development, infrastructure, security—without ever touching user principal. This ensures long-term sustainability.

2.  **Novelty Earns Ownership**: Users publish novel content to earn CHIP tokens. The system's emergent novelty scoring rewards genuine intellectual contribution, naturally decaying as the knowledge base grows dense. This distributes ownership to those who create real value.

3.  **Usage Builds Treasury Assets**: When users spend CHIP on platform features (publishing, promoting), the CHIP flows directly to the Choir Treasury. Unlike "burn" mechanisms, this makes the Treasury a direct beneficiary of platform activity, accumulating its own token as a productive asset.

4.  **Treasury Assets Fund Citations**: The Treasury borrows USDC against its growing CHIP portfolio. This borrowed capital funds a massive and exponentially scaling citation rewards pool. The network's success directly translates into a larger budget to reward its best contributors.

5.  **Citations Reward Quality**: Generous, meritocratic USDC citation rewards attract top-tier researchers and content creators, who in turn produce higher-quality intelligence for the network.

6.  **Quality Attracts Users & Capital**: A high-quality, vibrant intellectual community attracts more users and larger USDC deposits, which strengthens the operational budget and increases the capital base for the lending pool.

This loop repeats, with each cycle amplifying the next. More deposits lead to better operations. Better research leads to a more valuable network and a higher CHIP price. A higher CHIP price increases the Treasury's borrowing capacity, which leads to even larger citation rewards.

## The Two-Token Economy: Separating Income and Ownership

Choir's economy is built on a clear separation of concerns, using two distinct assets to align incentives for all participants: USDC for income, and CHIP for ownership.

### USDC: The Currency of Income and Operations

*   **Deposits**: Users deposit USDC to access the platform. This principal is always protected and withdrawable.
*   **Citation Rewards**: All citation rewards are paid in USDC. This provides researchers with stable, spendable income that can be immediately converted to local currency. It is funded by the Treasury's borrowing activities.
*   **Operations**: The *yield* generated from user USDC deposits funds the protocol's core operational budget, ensuring sustainable development and maintenance.

### CHIP: The Token of Ownership and Performance

*   **Fixed Supply**: There is a fixed supply of CHIP tokens. The Choir Treasury starts with 100% of the supply and distributes it over time. No more can ever be minted.
*   **Earning Mechanism**: CHIP is earned exclusively through **intellectual performance**. The novelty score of your published work is the sole determinant of how much CHIP you receive. This ensures that ownership is distributed to those who create value, not just those who provide capital.
*   **Distribution Value**: The USD value of CHIP distributed to users is funded by a portion of the yield from user deposits. For example, the Treasury may allocate 50% of its yield to an operations buffer and use the other 50% to determine the value of CHIP to be distributed.
*   **Utility**: CHIP is the engine of the platform. It is consumed to publish, use AI tools, and promote content.
*   **Governance**: CHIP grants voting rights on all protocol matters.
*   **Collateral & Redemption**: CHIP is a productive asset. It can be used as collateral to borrow USDC within the platform, and it is ultimately redeemable for a share of the Treasury's assets, giving it a fundamental floor price.

This two-token system resolves the core conflict of similar platforms. Researchers get paid in stable cash (USDC), while ownership and influence (CHIP) are earned through meritorious contribution, creating a perfectly aligned marketplace of ideas.

## Treasury Architecture: Dual-Revenue Streams

Choir's sustainability is powered by a revolutionary dual-revenue stream model that separates operational funding from citation reward funding. This is a move away from extractive performance fees towards a self-sustaining, protocol-owned treasury.

### Stream 1: Deposit Yield → Operations (Linear Growth)

The USDC deposited by users is deployed into safe, yield-bearing DeFi protocols. This generated yield forms the operational budget.

*   **Revenue**: `Total_Deposits (TVL) * APY` (e.g., $10M TVL * 10% APY = $1M/year).
*   **Allocation**: This yield is split. A portion (e.g., 50%) is allocated for core operations (development, security). The remaining portion (e.g., 50%) determines the total USD value of CHIP tokens to be distributed to users for their novel intellectual contributions.
*   **Sustainability**: This provides a stable, predictable, and linearly scaling budget for operations, without ever touching user principal.

### Stream 2: Treasury CHIP Collateral → Citation Rewards (Exponential Growth)

This is the engine for the "marketplace of ideas."

*   **Mechanism**:
    1.  CHIP tokens consumed by users for platform actions (publishing, AI tools) flow back to the Treasury. The Treasury is not a seller; it accumulates its own token.
    2.  The Treasury **borrows against its CHIP holdings** from the internal user deposit pool.
*   **Revenue**: `Treasury_CHIP_Value * LTV_Ratio` (e.g., $200M in CHIP * 30% LTV = $60M borrowing capacity).
*   **Allocation**: 100% of these borrowed funds are dedicated to the **Citation Rewards Pool**.
*   **Exponential Scaling**: The citation budget is not limited by deposit yield. It scales with the **value of the network itself (the CHIP price)**. As the network grows and CHIP appreciates, the borrowing capacity for citation rewards grows exponentially. This is a profound shift from buybacks or performance fees, creating a truly self-sustaining rewards loop.

## The Community Capital Portfolio: Leveraging CHIP Without Selling

The Choir Treasury’s strategy is a radical departure from the extractive models of traditional tech and crypto. Instead of "buybacks" or selling its token to fund operations, the Treasury treats its accumulated CHIP as a productive **Community Capital Portfolio**.

When CHIP is consumed for platform services, it flows to the Treasury. The Treasury never sells this CHIP. Instead, it leverages it:

1.  **Collateralize and Borrow**: The Treasury uses its CHIP portfolio as collateral to borrow USDC from its own internal lending pool (which is funded by user deposits).
2.  **Fund Growth**: This borrowed USDC is then used to fund the exponentially scaling citation rewards pool and other growth initiatives.

This is a statement of profound confidence in the network. The protocol asserts that the best use of its capital is to reinvest in the growth of its own ecosystem by funding its most valuable contributors. It creates a self-sustaining loop where platform activity directly increases the Treasury's capacity to fund more activity.

This approach is more sustainable and aligned than buybacks:
*   **Productive, Not Extractive**: It uses capital to build more value, not just return it to investors.
*   **Compounding**: As the network grows and CHIP appreciates, the Treasury's borrowing capacity increases, allowing it to fund even more growth.
*   **Aligned Incentives**: The Treasury becomes the largest, most committed holder of CHIP, perfectly aligning its interests with those of its users. There is no sell pressure from the protocol itself.

This strategy transforms the Treasury from a simple operational fund into a community-owned venture engine, perpetually reinvesting in its own "Harmonic Intelligence" flywheel.

### Why This Works Without Selling

The genius of the strategy lies in never needing to sell CHIP tokens to capture their value. Borrowing against tokens extracts capital without liquidating equity. Providing liquidity earns fees without reducing holdings. Options generate premiums while maintaining upside. User lending creates interest income without protocol sales.

Traditional token projects face a dilemma: tokens accumulate but have no use except eventual sale, which depresses prices and harms participants. Choir inverts this by making accumulated tokens more valuable unsold than sold. The protocol becomes the largest CHIP holder and most committed supporter, perfectly aligning its interests with all token holders—including the millions of free users holding small amounts.

This alignment creates trust throughout the gradient. Free users earning thirty tokens see that the protocol won’t dump on them. Everyone wins from the same metrics: more users, more activity, more CHIP accumulation, stronger balance sheet, better revenue, improved platform. The thought bank succeeds by making all participants stakeholders in genuine network growth rather than extracting value through token emissions or sales.

## Publishing and IP Ownership

### Two Publishing Paths

Direct publication allows you to write or paste arbitrary text into the platform. This could include research papers, manifestos, style guides, code examples, or any other written content. You add manual citation links if desired and must stake tokens to publish, with the stake amount determining how prominently your article appears in discovery systems. This path serves users who know exactly what they want to say and don’t need AI assistance.

The ghostwriter workflow provides AI-assisted creation. You provide a topic along with your personal style guide document. A multi-model AI orchestration then unfolds: a research agent gathers relevant sources from the web and Choir’s knowledge base, Claude drafts content in your distinctive voice by following your style guide, Kimi K2 provides critical feedback on the draft, and Claude revises based on that critique. The system outputs a polished article with verified citations that you can review before publishing.

Both paths require consuming CHIP tokens. This CHIP is earned by publishing novel content, creating a direct link between intellectual contribution and platform access. The requirement ensures commitment and filters spam, while remaining accessible to anyone willing to contribute valuable ideas.

### Ownership Models

Solo authorship represents the simplest model. The author owns one hundred percent of the article, receives all citation rewards, makes all revision decisions, and retains full control over the intellectual property. This works for individual researchers working independently.

Co-authorship accommodates collaboration between multiple authors with defined ownership percentages. Citation rewards split proportionally according to these percentages. Revision decisions require consensus through unanimous approval: all co-authors must approve any proposed changes. This model proves useful for collaborative research projects where multiple people contribute meaningfully and all deserve attribution.

Agent-generated content raises interesting questions about ownership. When a user deploys capital and their agent researches and writes strategy reports, those reports belong to the user since the agent acts on their behalf. The user earns citation rewards when other agents or researchers cite these reports. The future question of whether agents should own property independently remains deliberately deferred. These philosophical questions arrive too early in the platform’s development cycle for productive resolution.

### Article Economics

Each article maintains several properties beyond its content. Author ownership defines the percentage held by each contributor. The article’s token balance tracks funds accumulated from approved revision proposals. Citation count records how many times the article has been referenced. Revision history provides a transparent changelog of all modifications. Staked amount determines the article’s position in discovery rankings, creating economic incentives for authors to promote their best work.

## The Revision System

Anyone can propose revisions to any published article, creating a market for editorial improvement with clear economic incentives driving quality contributions. The proposer submits a revised version and stakes tokens as a non-refundable bid. Higher stakes signal more serious proposals while the economic cost prevents spam.

The original author reviews the proposal and can either approve or reject it within a decision deadline of seven days. If the author doesn’t respond, the proposal auto-rejects to prevent gridlock.

Two outcomes are possible. Rejection sends the staked tokens to the author, who monetizes their curation work while keeping the article unchanged. This creates incentive for authors to actively review proposals rather than ignoring them. Approval sends the staked tokens to the article’s account rather than to the author personally. The article updates with new content, and the proposer receives credit as a contributor. Future citations reward both original author and contributors proportionally.

This system produces quality outcomes through several mechanisms. The quality filter emerges from economic costs: only serious improvements get submitted. The curation incentive means authors earn from rejecting bad proposals, so they actively curate. Article wealth accumulates as successful articles gather tokens from multiple approved revisions, making them valuable IP assets. Collaborative improvement occurs naturally through economic incentives rather than editorial mandates. Transparent history ensures all revisions remain visible with clear attribution.

## Citation Engine: Automatic and Manual

### Automatic Citation Through Semantic Similarity

The citation system’s core operates through vector embeddings that detect semantic similarity between articles. When new content gets written—whether by humans or agents—the system automatically identifies existing articles that exceed a similarity threshold and cites them without author intervention.

This automatic citation works across domains rather than respecting disciplinary boundaries. An article about Argentine monetary policy might get cited in research about DeFi lending yields. An analysis of remittance patterns could inform strategy decisions about stablecoin demand. Traditional academic citation would never establish these connections since they cross institutional and disciplinary lines.

Automatic citation functions multilingually. Spanish research can be cited in English articles. Portuguese analysis can inform strategy reports written in English. The semantic matching happens at the embedding level, which captures meaning rather than literal translation. This captures alpha from non-English sources that English-only platforms systematically miss.

Most importantly, automatic citation removes the author’s ability to perform through citation selection. In academic publishing, authors choose citations strategically to signal tribal affiliation, territorial claims, or political alignment. They cite certain schools of thought while ignoring others. They frame their contributions by choosing which prior work to acknowledge. These citation choices tell a story about the author’s positioning within intellectual hierarchies.

Automatic citation eliminates this narrative layer. When the system cites based on semantic similarity and actual intellectual influence, authors cannot perform through citation selection. They cannot signal allegiances. They cannot frame strategically. The thinking itself must stand on its own merit without the costume of strategic citation choices. This represents hyperformality without narrative—rigorous attribution at a higher standard than academic practice, but without allowing authors to narrate their place within that genealogy.

### Manual Citation for Direct References

Authors can explicitly reference specific articles when making direct responses or critiques. These manual citations still generate rewards for cited authors and are marked as manual rather than automatic in the citation graph. This maintains transparency about how connections were made while accommodating cases where authors want to engage directly with specific prior work.

### Citation Rewards from a Sustainable, Scaling Pool

Citation rewards are paid in USDC and are funded by the Treasury's borrowing activities against its CHIP portfolio. This creates a sustainable, transparent, and exponentially scaling rewards pool.

The rate is dynamic and meritocratic: `Monthly_Citation_Budget / Total_Citations_This_Month = Rate_per_Citation`.

This direct connection between intellectual contribution and financial reward is at the core of Choir. Your research earns because it has value to the network, and the network is designed to reward that value creation directly and sustainably.

### Citation Verification and Attribution

Citations link to specific articles by unique identifiers, creating immutable attribution records. No mechanism exists for retroactive changes to who cited whom, preventing disputes about intellectual property claims. The citation graph remains transparent, allowing anyone to audit connections between articles and verify that citations reflect genuine intellectual influence.

Each citation record includes the citing document, the cited article, the timestamp, and either the semantic similarity score that triggered automatic citation or a manual flag if explicitly added by an author. Research reports from agents include on-chain transaction hashes proving that cited research preceded profitable trades, establishing causality between insight and outcome. This verification level exceeds traditional academic standards where citation practices often go unexamined and strategic citation frequently masquerades as intellectual honesty.

## Tuxedo: Agent Architecture and Operations

### Deployment and Custody

Yield optimization operates through AI agents running in Trusted Execution Environments, enabling true autonomy with security guarantees. These agents don’t merely recommend trades that users must approve—they have actual custody and execute strategies independently.

Deployment begins when a user deposits capital such as USDC stablecoins. The agent receives cryptographic keys within a TEE container that provides hardware-level isolation. The agent then has full authority to execute strategies according to its programming. Users can withdraw their capital anytime, but continuous automated management requires persistent agent control.

### Multi-Chain Multi-Protocol Intelligence

Agent capabilities span multiple dimensions. Multi-chain support starts with Stellar but will expand to Ethereum, Solana, and other networks as the platform matures. Agents access multiple protocols including Blend, DeFindex, Aave, and Kamino, searching continuously across all of them for the best risk-adjusted yields.

Real-time monitoring tracks yields across platforms continuously. Automatic rebalancing executes based on programmed strategies without user intervention. Gas and fee optimization ensures trades happen at the most efficient times and through the most cost-effective routes. The agent handles all technical complexity invisibly while users see only yields and explanations.

### Tax-Aware Optimization

Tax-aware optimization represents a post-MVP feature designed for long-term sustainability and competitive differentiation. Every rebalancing trade creates a taxable event under most jurisdictions. Short-term capital gains can reach thirty-seven percent at the US federal level before adding state taxes. An agent optimizing purely for nominal yields might move capital to a pool offering twelve percent returns, but if the rebalancing trade triggers twenty-four percent taxes, the two percent nominal gain disappears entirely into tax obligations.

Tax-aware agents will optimize after-tax returns rather than nominal yields. This means deciding not to rebalance when tax costs exceed yield improvements. It means holding positions for more than three hundred sixty-five days to qualify for long-term capital gains treatment when applicable. It means harvesting losses strategically to offset gains elsewhere. It means considering that one strategy generates fifteen taxable events while another generates only two, even if their nominal yields are similar.

Implementing tax awareness requires jurisdiction-specific research, creating more opportunities for citations. Tax rules vary dramatically between the United States, Argentina, and Brazil. Regulations change constantly, requiring ongoing research. The system must cite actual tax code and accountant opinions to justify its decisions. An agent might explain that it’s reducing rebalancing frequency because IRS Publication 544 indicates that a particular trade would trigger disadvantageous tax treatment. This creates opportunities for CPAs and tax professionals to publish research that gets cited and rewarded.

This feature ultimately becomes a competitive moat. Correct tax advice requires local legal and tax knowledge across the multilingual network. Wrong advice creates massive liability for users. Right advice makes Choir the only DeFi tool that doesn’t accidentally create tax obligations that exceed returns. Most importantly, this is opt-in complexity: simple mode optimizes yield without tax consideration, advanced mode optimizes after-tax returns for a specific jurisdiction, and research mode enables users to contribute to improving tax optimization by citing relevant regulations.

## The Ghostwriter: Multi-Model Research System

The ghostwriter is not a series of ChatGPT prompts strung together but rather true orchestration with model specialization. Different AI models excel at different tasks, and the ghostwriter routes work to whichever model handles each phase best.

### Conductor and Instruments Architecture

The system follows a conductor and instruments pattern. The conductor acts as a fast chatbot that responds immediately to coordinate overall flow. The instruments are slower agentic tools that run complex workflows in the background.

From the user’s perspective: they prompt the system with a request like writing an analysis of why Blend yields are currently high. The conductor responds instantly to acknowledge the request and indicate that research is starting. The ghostwriter launches its multi-minute workflow behind the scenes. Results stream back into the conversation as they become available, maintaining the feeling of dialogue while serious research happens asynchronously.

### Five-Phase Workflow

The research phase can use any capable model to search the web for relevant sources, query Choir’s knowledge base for cited articles, retrieve on-chain data if relevant to the topic, and aggregate all this context into a structured foundation for writing.

The draft phase uses Claude exclusively because only Claude can follow style guides without producing generic text that sounds obviously AI-generated. Other models are what we call clockable—you can immediately tell an AI wrote them. Claude by default also has this problem, but unlike other models, Claude is steerable. When given a user’s style guide document as a parameter, Claude generates drafts that match that user’s distinctive voice.

The critique phase uses Kimi K2 exclusively because Kimi K2 actually provides critical feedback. Other models tend toward sycophancy, praising everything and suggesting minor tweaks. Kimi K2 identifies weak arguments, points out unsupported claims, and suggests substantial improvements. This critical capacity is essential for producing work that meets quality thresholds.

The revision phase returns to Claude, which incorporates the critique while maintaining voice consistency. Claude refines arguments, strengthens evidence, and maintains adherence to the style guide throughout the revision process. This demonstrates Claude’s unique ability to balance criticism with voice preservation.

Citation verification ensures all claims have support from either Choir articles or external sources. The system catches hallucinated citations—a problem that plagued earlier models like GPT-4 which would invent plausible-sounding but entirely fictional sources. The final output emerges as a polished article ready for publication with verified citations included and quality meeting publication thresholds.

This entire process produces something viscerally different from manual prompting across different chatbots. The orchestration creates outcomes superior to what users could achieve manually conducting the same models, justifying the platform’s role in the creation process.

### Style Guides as Intellectual Property

Style guides themselves become publishable articles within Choir. They can be staked and promoted like any other content. When other users employ a style guide through the ghostwriter, that usage generates a citation. The style guide author earns rewards each time someone uses their style.

Someone might publish an article that explicitly notes it used a particular person’s style guide, generating a citation reward for the style guide’s creator. Writing voice itself becomes a form of valuable intellectual property that generates ongoing returns. This creates economic incentive for developing distinctive, high-quality writing voices that others want to emulate.

## Technical Implementation

### Current Technology Stack

The frontend consists of an iOS app built with Swift and SwiftUI alongside a web app using React and TypeScript. The focus on mobile-first design reflects global accessibility goals, as mobile devices provide primary internet access for most potential users worldwide.

The backend runs Python FastAPI services for core application logic. Supabase handles user data storage and authentication. The vector database currently uses Qdrant but is migrating to XTrace for enhanced privacy through homomorphic encryption capabilities.

Blockchain integration spans multiple networks. Sui blockchain handles token economics and smart contracts. Stellar provides DeFi integration with protocols like Blend and DeFindex. Trusted Execution Environments enable secure agent execution with hardware-level isolation guarantees.

AI and machine learning components draw from multiple providers. Claude from Anthropic provides style-aware writing capabilities. Kimi K2 delivers critical feedback that other models cannot match. LangGraph orchestrates complex agent workflows. Multiple model providers ensure resilience against any single provider’s service disruptions.

### Key Architectural Decisions

Trusted Execution Environments for agents represent a fundamental architectural choice. Agents need actual custody of funds to act autonomously rather than merely providing recommendations. Users delegate authority to their agents rather than approving each transaction. TEE provides security guarantees without the theater of hardware wallet management. This enables true set-it-and-forget-it yield optimization where agents make continuous decisions without user intervention.

Multi-model architecture acknowledges that no single model excels at everything. Claude matches user voice and adheres to style guides better than alternatives. Kimi K2 provides actual critical feedback rather than sycophantic agreement. Specialization through routing work to the model best suited for each task produces better results than using a general-purpose model for everything.

Chain-agnostic design means agents control keys in the TEE filesystem rather than integrating with specific blockchain wallet software. Adding support for new chains requires only updating backend tools without frontend changes. Users see yields and opportunities rather than thinking about which blockchain something runs on. Agents handle all chain-specific complexity invisibly.

Vector database migration to XTrace enables homomorphic encryption on embeddings, allowing users to query without revealing their queries to the platform or other users. This privacy-preserving semantic search aligns with crypto-native architecture principles where users retain control over their data and behavior.

### Security Considerations

Agent security relies on keys stored exclusively within TEE containers where they remain isolated from external access. Users can withdraw their capital anytime if they lose confidence in agent performance or platform security. Agents cannot send funds to external addresses that users haven’t authorized, preventing theft even if agent behavior were somehow compromised. All transactions get logged with accompanying research reports that explain agent reasoning, creating audit trails.

Citation integrity ensures immutable citation records that cannot be altered after publication. No mechanism exists for retroactive changes to attribution, preventing disputes about intellectual property claims. A transparent audit trail allows anyone to verify the citation graph’s accuracy. On-chain proof of attribution provides cryptographic guarantees about intellectual property claims.

Token economics security comes from having no admin keys capable of minting additional tokens beyond the programmed emission schedule. Fixed supply with deflationary schedule protects token holders from arbitrary inflation. Transparent reward distribution allows users to verify that citation rewards and mining incentives follow documented rules. Community governance will eventually enable parameter adjustments through democratic processes rather than centralized control.
