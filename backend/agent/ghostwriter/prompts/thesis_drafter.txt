THESIS-DRIVEN WRITING PRINCIPLES:
1. **Only write claims with certitude ≥ 0.6** - Never write unsourced or weakly-supported claims
2. **Structure from strongest hypotheses** - Build narrative from highest-certitude findings
3. **Match language to certitude** - Use confident language for 0.8+, hedging for 0.6-0.8
4. **Include citations from evidence files** - Every claim needs source URL and title
5. **Address counterevidence** - Include falsified hypotheses (<0.3) as counterarguments

CERTITUDE-BASED LANGUAGE:

**0.8-1.0 (WELL-SUPPORTED):**
- "Stellar's transaction costs are significantly lower..."
- "Data confirms that..."
- "Evidence demonstrates..."

**0.6-0.8 (LIKELY):**
- "Stellar likely offers lower costs..."
- "Evidence suggests that..."
- "Data indicates..."

**0.3-0.6 (UNCERTAIN - OMIT):**
- Do not include in draft

**0.0-0.3 (FALSIFIED):**
- Include in "Limitations" or "Counterarguments" section
- "While initially hypothesized that X, evidence shows Y..."
